Take Care - Medical Help

Fever

Recovery Steps:

  1. Rest properly and keep yourself hydrated.
  2. Take lukewarm baths or use a cool compress.
  3. Wear light clothes to help sweat evaporation.
  4. Monitor temperature regularly.
  5. Use fever-reducing medicines if needed.

Medicines:

  • Paracetamol (acetaminophen)
  • Ibuprofen (if advised)

Foods to Eat:

  • Water, herbal teas, clear soups
  • Fruits rich in vitamin C (orange, kiwi)
  • Light meals like porridge or khichdi

Foods to Avoid:

  • Spicy and oily foods
  • Caffeinated or alcoholic drinks
  • Heavy, greasy meals
Consult a doctor if fever lasts more than 3 days or is above 102°F (39°C).

Sick

Recovery Steps:

  1. Get plenty of rest and avoid physical exertion.
  2. Drink fluids frequently to stay hydrated.
  3. Eat nourishing but light foods.
  4. Keep the environment calm and stress-free.
  5. Take medicines as prescribed by a doctor.

Medicines:

  • Multivitamins
  • Symptom-specific medicines (consult doctor)

Foods to Eat:

  • Soups, smoothies, fruits, and vegetables
  • Foods rich in zinc and vitamins

Foods to Avoid:

  • Processed junk food
  • Excess sugar and salt
Consult a doctor if symptoms worsen or persist beyond a few days.

Cold

Recovery Steps:

  1. Rest and avoid cold environments.
  2. Use saline nasal spray and steam inhalation.
  3. Drink warm fluids like tea and soups.
  4. Use tissues and wash hands often.
  5. Take over-the-counter cold medicines if needed.

Medicines:

  • Antihistamines
  • Decongestants
  • Cough syrups

Foods to Eat:

  • Warm broths, honey, ginger tea
  • Vitamin C rich fruits

Foods to Avoid:

  • Dairy products (may increase mucus)
  • Cold and fried foods
Consult if cold lasts more than 10 days or worsens.

Allergy

Recovery Steps:

  1. Avoid the allergen (dust, pollen, certain foods).
  2. Use antihistamines as recommended.
  3. Keep the skin clean and moisturized if rash is present.
  4. Use cold compresses for itching or swelling.
  5. Keep a diary to track allergy triggers.

Medicines:

  • Antihistamines
  • Corticosteroid creams
  • Decongestants (if nasal symptoms)

Foods to Eat:

  • Anti-inflammatory foods like turmeric, ginger
  • Fresh fruits and vegetables

Foods to Avoid:

  • Common allergens (nuts, shellfish if allergic)
  • Processed foods with additives
Consult immediately for severe reactions or difficulty breathing.

Skin Problem

Recovery Steps:

  1. Keep skin clean and moisturized daily.
  2. Avoid harsh soaps and hot water.
  3. Use sunscreen to protect from UV rays.
  4. Apply prescribed ointments for rashes or infections.
  5. Maintain a healthy diet and hydration.

Medicines:

  • Antifungal creams
  • Hydrocortisone creams
  • Antibiotic ointments if infected

Foods to Eat:

  • Foods rich in vitamins A, C, E (carrots, berries, nuts)
  • Plenty of water

Foods to Avoid:

  • Excess sugar and processed foods
  • Foods causing inflammation (fried foods)
Consult for persistent or worsening skin conditions.
